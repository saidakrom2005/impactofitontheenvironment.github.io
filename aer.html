Research

Our team has made a research in some schools and even have recorded some kind of an interview in which it was told about some problems of the schools in remote area.

1. Limited Resources: Funding: Rural schools often receive less funding per student than urban schools, which can lead to a shortage of resources such as textbooks, technology, and classroom supplies. Facilities: Many rural schools have outdated or inadequate facilities, including inadequate heating and cooling systems, limited internet access, and crumbling infrastructure. Specialized services: Rural schools may not have access to specialized services such as speech therapy, occupational therapy, and mental health counseling, which can be crucial for students with special needs.

2. Lack of Access to Technology and Educational Materials: Limited internet access: Many rural areas lack access to high-speed internet, which can limit students' access to online resources and educational technology. Outdated textbooks and materials: Rural schools often have outdated textbooks and other educational materials, which can put students at a disadvantage compared to their peers in urban schools. Limited access to extracurricular activities: Rural schools may offer fewer extracurricular activities than urban schools, which can limit students' opportunities for social and emotional development.
