IT's Impact on Environment

Benefits, Drawbacks and Effects

Research

Our team has made a research in a field of IT and has defined its drawbacks and benefints which we will present now:

1. E-waste: Electronic waste, or e-waste, is the fastest-growing waste stream globally, with an estimated 53.6 million metric tons generated in 2019. Improper disposal of e-waste can release toxic substances like lead and mercury into the environment, causing serious health and environmental problems.
2. Resource extraction: Manufacturing IT devices requires mining for rare earth metals and other resources, which can lead to deforestation, soil erosion, water pollution, and biodiversity loss.

3. Opportunities for green IT: Despite the challenges, there are opportunities to mitigate the environmental impact of IT. These include promoting sustainable manufacturing practices, developing energy-efficient devices, and designing products for longer lifespans and easier recycling.

4. Circular economy: The circular economy approach aims to reduce waste and conserve resources by extending the lifespan of products and materials. This can be applied to IT through initiatives such as product repair, reuse, and remanufacturing.
